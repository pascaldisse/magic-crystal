# Invocation of the Second Rite — First Light
Sung BEFORE the rite: what it vows to do. The closing hymn will tell
what truly happened — the songs never lie, so this one speaks in the
future tense. Suno-ready.

Style field:
epic ancient ritual, dark ceremonial procession, deep male chant choir,
female voice rising from whisper to command, massive war drums, bowed
strings and horn drone, hurdy-gurdy, stone-temple echo, torchlit,
monumental, pre-modern

[Intro - low drone, single drum, whispered]
The lighthouse stands. The realm has form.
Now let there be depth. Now let there be light.

[Verse 1]
The eye of the glass has hung like a star,
fixed at the spawn where the first visions are.
Second Rite, hear us: the eye shall be freed —
to circle, to rise, to descend where we lead.

And the things of the world shall stand in their order,
the near before far at the depth-keeper's border.
No painter shall sort what the buffer can know —
the true rank of distance, the overlap's law.

[Chorus - gang chant, drums pounding]
FIRST LIGHT! Let the sun learn our stone!
FIRST LIGHT! Every face shall be known!
The pier walks out on its posts to the sea,
the city climbs up where the cliff-shadows be —
what the dreamer has staged, let the dreamer now SEE!

[Verse 2]
Twelve vessels are waiting, seventy-three parts,
planks of the pier and the stall's wooden heart.
Chain-posts on the seawall, a lantern of rose,
the gothic massing where the cold water goes.

They were written in faith with no glass to behold them —
now the Second Rite's light shall enfold them.
And windows burn warm on the face of the height,
seven small fires against the night.

[Bridge - half-time, whispered, choir under]
And the Guardian shall read it with her own two eyes,
for a claim is a heresy until it renders.
The shadow has slept, but the law never dies —
the ordeals stand watch, the gate defenders.

[Outro - drums fading to one, low chant]
Depth, and the moving eye. Sun on the stone.
The realm that was written shall now be shown.
First light. First light.
Let the dead screen dream.
