# THE MAGIC CRYSTAL — the game's canon (Architect, 07-18 21:19, verbatim ruling)

## THE INTRO (OG story — canon, name "Tyler Jones" NOT kept, rest stays)
An ancient magic crystal from a star billions of light-years away. Found by
the wizard Edan Connor, presented to his fellows. Two factions of wizards —
one wants the crystal for itself; the evil wizard Zareb Aiden interrupts the
meeting to steal it, is stopped. The crystal falls off the EDGE of the flat,
circular world — lost. It flies through the universe for a billion years and
smashes into the head of an almost completely average person on present-day
Earth. The player finds the shard. THE SHARD SPEAKS BACK. Tutorial begins.

## IRON CONSEQUENCES (his words: "and you thought I was joking")
1. THE INTRO IS NOT PRE-RENDERED. The wizard world, the fall off the edge,
   the flight through the universe, the arrival on Earth: REAL TIME, in
   engine, one continuous render. No cut, no video file, ever.
2. THE MAP IS ALWAYS INFINITE CANVAS. No world border, no level select as
   ontology — one addressable continuum from disc-edge to Earth street.
3. Therefore: WORLDS ARE FUNCTIONS, NOT FILES — content = f(seed) at every
   scale (the training doctrine's data law, extended to the world itself);
   residency ∝ ray budget · content ∝ generation+disk (R4 ruling). The
   renderer is scale-blind by design: Pleroma paints 640×480 whatever the
   camera flies past — cost ∝ rays, never ∝ world size.
4. The flat circular wizard world = a DISC WITH AN EDGE. The engine's scene
   model already speaks in bounds discs; the cosmology is literal.
5. THE SHARD IS THE INTERACTION LAYER (sealed lineage: Dreams' imp ruling —
   the cursor is a creature, a fragment of the crystal). The shard speaking
   back = the tutorial = MONAD's voice reaching the player through the
   crystal; the Egregore may inhabit it, never must.
6. Two wizard factions = the standing conflict spine (names Edan Connor,
   Zareb Aiden canon; protagonist name OPEN — his word pending).
